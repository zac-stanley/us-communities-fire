About this map

This map shows the intersection of wildfire hazard potential and socially vulnerable communities throughout the conterminous United States.

Combining two symbology techniques; sequential colors and proportional symbols the map is a bivariate representation of census designated places with two phenomena simultaneously. The sequential colors represent wildfire hazard potential: moderate, high and very high. The proportional symbols represent the level of social vulnerability: 0-1.

How to use the map

Hover over a point of interest to get retrieve the community name, its wildfire hazard potential class and an overall social vulnerability score (SVI)

Click on a point of interest to zoom to that location see the community boundary with wildfire hazard potential colors a detailed breakdown of the individual values that make up the overall SVI value. You can also use the search bar in the upper right of the map to zoom to a community boundary and get the same detailed break you get by clicking on a point.
